### Question 14

Risk factors for pneumonia in children are classified into definite, probable and possible risk factors
Which of the following is a probable risk factor?

Select one:

a. high altitude

b. Malnutrition 

c. Low birth weight

d. incomplete immunization 

e. Household air pollution


<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>
b. Malnutrition 

<p> Definite risk factors are those that have a proven association with the occurrence of pneumonia. Possible risk factors are those that have been suggested as risk factors, but the evidence is not strong enough to establish a causal relationship. Probable risk factors are those that have a plausible biological association with pneumonia and are supported by some evidence, but the evidence is not as strong as for definite risk factors.</p>
</details>
