### Question 8

Select one:

a. Taenia capitis

b. Diarrhoeal diseases

c. Bilharziasis

d. Hook worm infection

e. Malaria

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>
c. Bilharziasis

<p>Water-borne: contaminated drinking water, diarrhea. Water-washed: due to lack of proper sanitation and hygiene eg taenia, trachoma, ascariasis, and ancylostomiasis. Water-based diseases: transmitted through an aquatic invertebrate organism such as schistosomiasis. Vector-borne: transimitted through insects that depend on water for propagation eg malaria, lymphatic filariasis, onchocerciasis. Water-dispersed: legionella.</p>
</details>

### Question 18

Which of the following concerning measles vaccine is TRUE?

Select one:

a. It is contraindicated in HIV infected children

b. It may be stored at +2°C to +8°C at regional vaccine stores

c. Vaccine vial monitor is found on the side of the vial

d. Shake test is useful in determining potency

e. Contraindicated in egg allergy

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>
b. It may be stored at +2°C to +8°C at regional vaccine stores

Can be stored at -15 to -25°C or +2°C to +8°C 

Measles vaccines are recommended for use in HIV-infected individuals unless severely immunocompromised. A systematic review and meta-analysis of the safety and immunogenicity of measles vaccine in HIV-infected children commissioned by the WHO Global Advisory Committee on Vaccine Safety (GACVS)47 reported that there was no increased risk of serious adverse events among HIV-infected children when compared with uninfected children. 

<p>MMR vaccine is contraindicated in children known to be allergic to eggs because the mumps virus strain is cultured on embryonated chicken eggs. Other vaccine that is contraindicated is yellow fever vaccine</p>
</details>
