### Question 26 

Which of the following concerning anthropometric measurements in children is FALSE?

a. In infants weight measurement must be read to the nearest 0.01kg.

b. Head circumference measurement increases by 16 cm in the first 3 years of life.

c. Birth length doubles by the age of 4 years.

d. If the accurate age of a child is not available, one should record the height measurement and not the length measurement if the child measures approximately 75 cm.

e. When using an infantometer the movable piece is the leg board.

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

d. If the accurate age of a child is not available, one should record the height measurement and not the length measurement if the child measures approximately 75 cm. 

Cut off point for length/height is 87cm. If the child is less than 87cm, use length board(infantometer). If the child is more than 87cm, use height board(stadiometer).

</details>
