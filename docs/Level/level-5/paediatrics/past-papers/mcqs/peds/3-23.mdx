### Question 47

The jugulovenous pressure:

Select one:

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

e. Irregular cannon waves indicate complete heart block.

Giant a wave forceful atrial contraction against a stenosed tricuspid valve or a non-compliant hypertrophied right ventricle produces an unusually prominent a wave.

Cannon a wave caused by atrial systole against a closed tricuspid valve. It occurs when atrial and ventricular rhythms are dissociated (complete heart block, ventricular tachycardia) and marks coincident atrial and ventricular systole

Giant v wave important sign of tricuspid regurgitation.

Prominent x and y descents occur in constrictive pericarditis. In tamponade only the x descent is usually exaggerated.

</details>
