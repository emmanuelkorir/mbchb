### Question 9 

A 18-month old baby presents with recurrent episodes of excessive crying followed by cyanosis, unconsciousness and occasional seizures since 9 months of age. The most likely diagnosis is:

a. Anoxic spells.

b. Vasovagal attack.

c. Breath holding spells.

d. Epilepsy.
 
<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

c. Breath holding spells.

A breath-holding spell is a reflexive response to pain, fear, or frustration. It is characterized by a brief period of apnea, which may be followed by cyanosis or pallor, and occasionally by loss of consciousness and seizures. The spells are usually precipitated by minor trauma or emotional upset. The spells are usually benign and self-limiting. Occur between 6 - 18 months.

Reflex anoxic seizures occur when the child is startled. The vagus nerve sends strong signals to the heart that causes it to stop beating. The child will suddenly become pale, lose consciousness and start to have some seizure-like muscle twitching.

Vasovagal attack/syncope - emotional or environmental trigger (prolonged standing, fasting, dehydration) causes vasovagal stimulation (bradycardia & peripheral vasodilation), drop in blood pressure and cerebral hypoperfusion. 

</details>

Which of the following is NOT a feature of Pertussis?:

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

Catarrhal stage (1-2 weeks): cough, watery discharge, low grade fever, conjunctivitis

Paroxysmal stage (2-6 weeks): high-pitched whooping cough, post-tussive vomiting, potential bleeding of the conjunctiva(due to increased intrathoracic pressure caused by coughing attacks)
