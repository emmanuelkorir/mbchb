### Question 17

The following are causes of industrial fire EXCEPT: -

Select one:

a. Non-adherence to chemical safety requirements

b. Poor housekeeping

c. Poor lighting

d. Inflammable liquids

e. Contactors' maintenance operations at the workplace

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

c. Poor lighting

Non-adherence to chemical safety requirements. This can include using chemicals in ways that are not approved by the manufacturer, storing chemicals in the wrong way, or failing to properly dispose of chemicals.

Poor housekeeping. This can include leaving flammable materials in areas where they can be ignited, such as near heat sources or electrical equipment.

Inflammable liquids. These are liquids that can easily catch fire, such as gasoline, paint thinner, and kerosene.

Contactors' maintenance operations at the workplace. This can include activities such as welding, grinding, and brazing, which can create sparks or flames that can ignite flammable materials.
</details>
