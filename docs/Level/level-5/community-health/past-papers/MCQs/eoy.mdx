### Question 21

Which one of the following statements regarding cohort studies is **TRUE**?

Select one:

A. A prospective cohort study is more efficient than a retrospective cohort study for studying diseases with a long latent and induction period.

B. Loss to follow-up can be a problem in a cohort study but not an experimental study.

C. Cohort studies are inherently superior to case-control studies.

D. The ideal comparison group for a cohort study would consist of exactly the same individuals in the exposed group had they not been exposed.

E. Cohort studies are the most sensible design for examining many exposures in relation to a single disease.

<details>
  <summary>Check Answer</summary>
  B. Loss to follow-up can be a problem in a cohort study but not an experimental
  study. Cohort studies involve following a group of individuals over time
  and tracking their outcomes, which can lead to loss to follow-up due
  to participants dropping out or being lost to the study. In contrast,
  experimental studies involve the random assignment of participants to
  different treatment groups, which eliminates the potential for loss to
  follow-up.
</details>
