Mary Makau at 24 weeks gestation by date, came to the antenatal clinic for routine follow up. On examination it was noted that her fundal height was at 28 weeks. She also reports that she had previous deliveries with babies whose weight: were all above 4.1 kg. A random blood sugar done revealed blood glucose level of 10.1 mg/dL

1. What is the likely diagnosis? (5 marks)

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

Para 3 +0 g4 with fundal height discrepancy?macrosomia and likely gestational diabetes

</details>

3. What lab tests would be important in this case - justify (20 marks)

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

TBC - to check for infection, anemia

FBS - to check state of her metabolism in relation to blood glucose levels

OGTT - to confirm level of blood sugar

Hba1c - help know how well blood sugar control has been over the past 3 months

Lipid profile - to check on metabolic state of patient especially in relation to her blood sugars

Antenatal profile - to ensure that the pregnancy is progressing well 

Obstetric ultrasound - to confirm the weight of fetus and to look for any abnormalities (polyhydramnios)

Urinalysis - check on effect of the elevated blood sugar on kidney function

UECS - check on effect of the elevated blood sugar on kidney function

Fundoscopy - look at retinopathy

</details>

4. She is recommended to have a screening test to check for diabetes? Kindly explain as to how this screening test will be carried out (10 marks)

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

75g/100g OGTT 

Patient starves from midnight and then comes to the lab in the morning.

Fasting blood sugar is taken then a glucose load in 50 ml water is given to be taken over 5 min.

Patient blood levels are tested at 1 hr, 2 hrs and in some cases three hours.

The results are put in a graph for easy referencing

</details>

6. She is managed in the clinic up to 38 weeks then she is induced. However when she gets to second stage the midwife notices that the baby's head would retract back in after crowning.

a. What diagnosis is made then? (5 marks)

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

Shoulder dystocia in a known diabetic in second stage of labor

</details>

b. Elucidate the steps that would be taken to resolve this situation. (10 marks)

<details>

- H - Call for help

- R- Roll patient to hand and knees

If all fails

</details>

7. After the successful extraction of a 4.7 kg baby, the patient suddenly gets a heavy gush of blood after delivery of the placenta. This is quantified as approximately 700ml blood loss. Kindly identify key management principles of the above issue (10 marks)

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

Primary PH likely secondary to Uterine atony
- Call for help
- Fixing 2 iv cannulae large bore
- IV fluid
- Group and match
- Catheterize
- Uterine massage
- Uterotonics - misoprostol, TRA
- Utenne
- Balloon tamponade
- If unremitting - surgical options including b lynch suture and hysterectomy.

</details>
