Lucy is a 33 year old para 2+1 gravida 4 who is brought to labor ward accompanied by her boyfriend with complaints of per vaginal drainage of
fluid for the last 2 days. Lucy says she is at about 35 weeks of gestation.
1. What is the most likely comprehensive diagnosis? Justify (4 marks)
2. List the most likely differential diagnosis (10 marks) 
3. What additional history would you obtain. (20 marks)
4. What examination would you perform and what are the likely findings
based on the above differential diagnosis (20 Marks)
5. Justify the relevant investigations you would order (18 marks)
6. On further inquiry she tells you she has had two prior cesarean deliveries and now has chills and rigors. The nurse tells you the temperature is 39 degrees celsius and the pulse rate is 114 beats per minute. Rationalize the general and specific management you will institute for Lucy (28 marks)


Judy Muthoni a para 6+0 gravida 7 at 32 weeks gestation, presents with a 5 day history of
dysuria, frequency and urgency of micturition for which she received oral antibiotic in a nearby clinic. In the last two days she's noted hotness of the body, headache, abdominal pain, occasional chills and rigors.
1. What is the most likely comprehensive diagnosis? (3 marks)
2. Give at least 5 differential diagnoses? (5 marks)
3. What additional history would you obtain from her? (20 marks)
4. Justify the investigations would you conduct? (24 marks)
5. What are the most likely causative organisms of the diagnosis? (8 marks)
6. What are the potential complications and the underlying pathology of the above diagnosis? (20 marks)
7. Outline the general and specific management plans for the patient with the diagnosis
identified in question 1 above (20 marks)
