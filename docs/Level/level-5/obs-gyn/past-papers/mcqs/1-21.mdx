### Question 12 

During puberty, which of the following is the last event 

a. Adrenarche 

b. Menarche 

c. All occur at the same time 

d. pubarche 

e. Thelarche

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

b. Menarche

In girls the first event is thelarche(the development of breasts), followed by pubarche(the development of axillary and pubic hair, it is considered a manifestation of adrenache), and then menarche(first menstrual period)

Normal order of changes: adrenarche → gonadarche → thelarche (age of onset 8–11 years) → growth spurt (age of onset 11.5–16.5 years) → pubarche (mean age of onset 12 years) → menarche (age of onset 10–16 years, mean age: 13 years)

### Question 15 

In a patient with right sided cancer of the vulva, the most likely site of first nodal metastasis 

a. Right femoral (deep) lymph nodes 

b. Para-aortic lymph nodes 

c. Right inguinal (superficial) lymph nodes 

d. Right obturator node 

e. Bilateral inguinal (superficial) lymph nodes 

<details>
<summary>Check Answer</summary>

c. Right inguinal (superficial) lymph nodes

Superficial inguinal lymph nodes: the horizontal group lies along the inguinal ligament: drain the inguinal region (inferior abdominal wall), gluteal region, perineum, scrotum, distal third vagina, vulva, lower anal canal (below the dentate line). The vertical group lies along the terminal part of the long saphenous vein: drain the lower extremity. Drain into deep inguinal and external iliac lymph nodes.

Deep inguinal lymph nodes: Receive lymph from the popliteal lymph nodes (that drain the leg and foot), the glans penis or clitoris, and the superficial lymph nodes. Drain into the external iliac lymph nodes

Upper third vagina: external iliac nodes. Middle third vagina: common and internal iliac nodes. Lower third: superficial inguinal. Ovaries - para-aortic nodes

</details>
